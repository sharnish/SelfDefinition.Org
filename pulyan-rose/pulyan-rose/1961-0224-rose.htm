From Rose

1674 High St., Benwood, W.Va.

Feb. 24, 1961

Friend Al,

But how can I be sure you are a friend? For all I know it might be something entirely different writing... hiding behind the postage stamp... behind space. I would have like to have been a little more scientific about your claims... investigated them first hand. But I recognized that while you might have something to fear or hide from a frustrated neophyte, I recognized the possible truth that I might not be able to tell much actually from first hand analysis.

You are unfair. This is by your own words. You resent and hint anathema at such trifling things as my caution, and your style of writing is so vitriolic that I wonder that anyone could insult you. Yet I hesitated to say anything that might hurt your feelings. I have commented about the reasonableness of statements but there has been no intention to tell you off.

I am tempted though. Surely you are not so naive as to think that I should accept everything anyone says. And there is no point in threatening to tell me to go to hell just because I do not agree.

In fact those are the little things that cast the doubt in my mind, more than the wealth or lack of reason. I can understand that you would want to share an experience if you had something bid, but when peoople get big they just do not have the urge to tell the less fortunate to go to hell. And I presume that is what you mean when you say you are tempted to say the obvious.

Almost grudgingly now you tell me that the mind lives unendingly. How do you know? (written in longhand)

Why? How do I know that you know. What are jivan-muktis? You do a little name-dropping. If I thought anything could be deduced from reading about Jesus or Buddha (since they wrote nothing) I would not be writing to you. I cannot ascertain if they existed. The Catholics claim that their people run clear back to Peter, but that claim does not mean a thing to me. I do not have the time to prove it, nor to prove the true origin of the Masons... since I have no assurance that knowing the unbroken chain of heredity would provide any qualitative knowledge of the first link.

Sometimes I think you are just an overgrown beatnik with a Russian sense of humor. Do you mean that your wife does not believe in the magnitude of your nothingness? My wife (amazingly different) knows me to be a hypocrite that I am. I keep telling her that I am but a mite of the Almighty and I get the same reaction that I get from you. "You are just saying this because it sounds big to make a claim that cannot be evaluated." Who knows? Perhaps my wife is a Zen master in disguise.

Of course the same applies to you. You defy evaluation. I am not as yet trying to evaluate you as much as to get some material to use in evaluating.

So far I get a picture of Atman and Brahma (Brahman?) Check me if I am wrong. Or is the little individual mind merging with the universal mind? Now if this is the case, does the individual (mind or spirit) lose its identity upon merging? Does it lose self-consciousness?

If it does not, and presuming you have attained the merger, Why have you not been able to identify dead relatives? If you knew everything, then why have you not able to discredit or explain other concepts and phenomena that lead men to believe other concepts?

What is the value of Kriya yoga technique. Is the Word of God (Logos) actually an audible sound?

Without asking for it I find that you find it fitting to again remind me that you would not want me to visit you. Now I ponder about this persistence. I realize that you all are transcendentalists and would have to run out and get a plumber to put in some plumbing to keep me from stinking up your place... no explanation is necessary. But then I also think (since I must be honest with you) that perhaps he is a little afraid that I am Tesla's pigeon... there is a light in my eye that you might not like.

You note that my correspondence is dull. At this point I note that you have the initiative.

What is a guru?

Sincerely,

Dick
